YouTube explainer covering latent-heat PCMs for residential and commercial thermal energy storage. Covers hot water (Sunamp Thermino), building insulation/HVAC supplements (BioPCM ENRG Blanket, QE Platinum), and market context. **Not about electronic/photonic PCMs.**

## Key Claims

- PCMs (paraffin, sodium acetate, hydrated salts) store latent heat at solid–liquid transitions; release heat during solidification.
- Sunamp Thermino: heat battery for hot water; 4× more compact than conventional tank; 50% less heat loss (0.74 vs. 1.3 kWh/day); saves up to 1,000 kWh/year; case study 59% energy reduction, 56% cost savings (~£602/yr).
- BioPCM ENRG Blanket (Phase Change Solutions): reduces HVAC power 25–35%, runtime 15–20%, cycling frequency 20–25%; ROI < 4 years for commercial buildings.
- PCM insulation costs ~$3.50/sq ft vs. $0.25–$1.40 for conventional foam.
- PCM market: $477M (2021) → ~$1B by 2026.
- Challenges: only effective in specific temperature range; can't adjust thermostat freely; heavier than standard insulation; eutectic vs. non-eutectic compositions affect thermal behavior.
- Bio-based and salt-hydrate PCMs offer cheaper alternatives to paraffin.
