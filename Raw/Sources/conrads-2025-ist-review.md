# Review: the plasmonic phase-change material In3SbTe2 for nanophotonics

Published in *Optical Materials Express*, Vol. 15, No. 11, 1 Nov 2025, pp. 2664–2687.
RWTH Aachen University, Institute of Physics (IA).

## Key claims

- IST (In3SbTe2) switches from an amorphous dielectric phase (ε′ ≈ +14) to a metallic crystalline phase (ε′ < 0) in the entire infrared spectral range — unlike conventional dielectric PCMs (GST alloys) which remain positive in both phases.
- The plasma frequency of crystalline IST is at 11111 cm⁻¹ (900 nm wavelength).
- IST is classified as a "bad metal" in the QTAIM bonding map, with electrical conductivity ~10⁴ S/cm.
- Crystal structure: cubic rocksalt, lattice parameter a = 6.126 Å. Controversy exists: crystallization may proceed via InSb + InTe at ~300°C followed by ternary IST at ~420°C, or directly to the ternary phase at 250°C.
- Thermal conductivity: amorphous 0.4–0.5 W/(K·m); crystalline 23 W/(K·m). Density: amorphous 6.1 g/cm³, crystalline 6.75 g/cm³.
- Optical switching parameters (660 nm laser, ~1 µm² spot): crystallization 5–60 mW / 200–2000 ns / 2–100 pulses; reamorphization 160–250 mW / 15–30 ns / 1 pulse.
- IST's tuning figure of merit (TFOM = Δν/FWHM) reaches up to 2.0 in slit antennas — exceeds the ~1 limit of dielectric PCMs.
- Direct laser writing enables antenna programming, emissivity-shaping metasurfaces, geometric phase metasurfaces (beam steering, lensing at 9 µm, vortex beams, holography), and polariton resonator control.
- Phase change is non-volatile — persists after cooling, unlike VO₂ (volatile insulator-to-metal transition at 68°C).
- IST on SiC supports programmable surface phonon polariton (SPhP) resonators with confinement λ/35.
