# Optical switching of GeSbTe-based Phase Change Superlattices with In₂Te₃

Bachelor's thesis by Ricco Widmann, RWTH Aachen University (I. Institute of Physics IA), May 2026. Samples grown by Lucas Bothe at the Helmholtz Nano Facility, Forschungszentrum Jülich.

## Key claims

- Introduces a **novel superlattice (SL) system**: GST-124 (Ge₁Sb₂Te₄, a *metavalent* PCM) alternating with In₂Te₃ (a *covalent* non-PCM). Prior SL studies used two metavalent PCMs (e.g. GST/Sb₂Te₃) — this creates a metavalent–covalent interface.
- **In₂Te₃** is covalently bonded, zinc-blende (space group F-43m), with one-third of indium sites intrinsically vacant (vacancy-rich, stabilizing); it does **not** exhibit phase-change behavior.
- Samples (`xI-yG`, GST fixed at 2.2 nm = "2G"; total film ~65 nm; Te-passivated Si(111); Sb₂Te₃ seed ~1–2 nm; ZnS:SiO₂ ~25 nm capping). Thinner In₂Te₃ ⇒ thinner supercell ⇒ more vdW-like interfaces.
- Local amorphization via pulsed laser (660 nm, LSS); characterized by optical microscopy, SEM, AFM, EBSD, Raman; XRD/XRR for structure.
- **PTE diagrams** map amorphized spot area vs. laser power and pulse duration. A 1.5 µm² area threshold separates true amorphization from ablation craters (AFM heights >125 nm exceed film thickness).
- **Sb₂Te₃ seed layer improves quality**: switching onset at lower pulse energy (confirmed by Laue fringes in XRD).
- **EBSD** confirms homogeneous amorphization in the 2I-2G SL; the pure In₂Te₃ reference shows only inhomogeneous structural change (amorphization excluded).
- **Main trend**: thinner supercells (more interfaces) switch at lower pulse energy with larger spots → improved switching efficiency. Not driven by optical absorption (Sb₂Te₃ absorbs more than In₂Te₃ at 660 nm) → structural origin.
- In₂Te₃-containing SLs show abrupt/sharp PTE transitions vs. smoother for conventional GST — possibly novel metavalent–covalent interface behavior.
- "White spots" appear for In₂Te₃ ≥ 6.6 nm: sub-threshold modification retaining crystalline-like Raman signature; origin unclear.
- **Annealing (250/300/350 °C, 2 h each)**: new XRD peak ~13° at ≥300 °C attributed to **InSb** (intermixing of In₂Te₃ with the Sb₂Te₃ seed; Sb₂Te₃ peak vanishes). Annealing shifts switching to higher pulse energy and smooths the transition → **decreased energy efficiency** (worse with higher T). Stress relaxation also contributes to smoother transitions.
- Formation energies (Materials Project): InSb (−0.238 eV/atom) less stable than In₂Te₃ (−0.405), Sb₂Te₃ (−0.383), GST-124 (−0.353); metastable phases still possible via Te out-diffusion.
- GST-124/In₂Te₃ SLs do **not** form conventional vdW heterointerfaces → switching cannot be explained by electro-thermal confinement alone.
- **Recrystallization not yet achieved** — identified as the key next step toward a full switching cycle. Proposed follow-ups: TEM, APT, SNOM.
