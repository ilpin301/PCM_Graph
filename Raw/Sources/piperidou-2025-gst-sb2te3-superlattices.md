# Investigating Interfacial Mechanisms and Switching Behavior in Ge₁Sb₂Te₄ / Sb₂Te₃ Superlattices

Master's thesis by Athanasia Piperidou, RWTH Aachen University (Institute IA), 2025.

## Key claims

- Studies **GST-124 (Ge₁Sb₂Te₄) / Sb₂Te₃ superlattices (SLs)** — both metavalent PCMs — across four configurations **N:2, N:4, N:6, N:8** (labelled by Sb₂Te₃ thickness; N:2 = thinnest supercell ~4.5 nm). GST-124 layer kept constant; total film ~60 nm; MBE-grown; ZnS:SiO₂ ~25 nm capping. References: pure GST-124 and pure Sb₂Te₃.
- Combines **Atom Probe Tomography (APT)**, laser switching (LSS, 660 nm), EBSD, AFM, optical microscopy.
- **PME (Probability of Multiple Events)** from APT is used as a **metavalent-bonding indicator**: high PME = metavalent (delocalized p-electrons), low PME = more covalent/localized.
- As-deposited APT: layering resolved (N:2 near APT resolution limit); APT vs. XRD supercell thickness agree within <0.6 nm. **Intermixing**: ~2–3 at.% Ge in Sb₂Te₃ layers; Ge in GST decreases N:2→N:8 (~6.5→~3.3 at.%) — asymmetric Ge interdiffusion; Te most stable.
- **Unconventional 2D defects** observed: layer-thickness variations, "glued"/merged GST layers, dissolved/incomplete layers, and atomic bridges — growth instabilities, not new phases.
- **Amorphization (PTE diagrams)**: performance order Sb₂Te₃ < GST-124 < superlattices (N:6 outlier). Among SLs, thinner supercell → larger amorphized spots → better efficiency. No correlation with total film thickness → intrinsic SL effect. Second amorphization cycle: same trends, slightly larger spots (analogous to electrical "forming").
- Reliable amorphization for APT: **N:8 at 170 mW / 16 ns**; AFM height increase ~10–12% (matches 6–10% PCM volume expansion); EBSD confirms amorphous.
- **APT of amorphized N:8**: layering completely lost; uniform composition Ge≈4%, Sb≈37%, Te≈59% (≈ Sb₂Te₃). PME drops 71% → 47% (still above ~20% typical of covalent PCMs → possible residual short-range order or APT artifact).
- **Recrystallization** (~20 pulses, 0–70 mW, 500–2000 ns): two regimes — nucleation-dominated (new ~90°-misoriented grains, lower power) and growth-dominated (front from matrix, orientation preserved, higher power). **N:2** recrystallizes by growth only and faster (~1400 ns); **N:8** behaves like GST-124 (both regimes, ~1900 ns).
- **APT of recrystallized SLs**: layered structure **restored** (N:2 ~8 layers; N:8 ~4 repetitions); PME recovers (N:2 ~62%, N:8 ~67%); stoichiometry shifts back toward nominal → partial self-organization on recrystallization.
- **Stochasticity**: near the amorphous→crystalline boundary, identical pulses (52 mW, varying duration) give a spread of outcomes → confirms stochastic nucleation.
- **Switching mechanism**: loss of layering on amorphization and its restoration on recrystallization strongly support the **melt-quench** mechanism over an order-to-order (crystal–crystal) transition.
- **Bond-confinement hypothesis** (central result): SLs show PME ~60–70% (vs. ~90% GST-124, ~86% Sb₂Te₃); thinner supercells → lower PME → more covalent character → more energy-efficient switching. This revises the earlier interface (vdW-gap) hypothesis. Causal link still open; proposed follow-ups: TEM, phonon (fs pump–probe), thermal conductivity (FDTR), DFT.
- Demonstrates APT viability for sub-5 nm engineered SLs and that SLs ~60 nm thick achieve performance gains close to real device dimensions.
